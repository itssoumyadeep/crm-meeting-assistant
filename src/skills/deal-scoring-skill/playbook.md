# Deal Scoring Playbook

This playbook outlines standard definitions for scoring CRM opportunities based on conversation metadata.

## 1. Buying Signals
- **Strong Signals**:
  - Direct budget confirmation (e.g., "We have $50k allocated for this").
  - Clear decision-maker presence (e.g., "The VP is sponsoring this project").
  - Concrete timeline (e.g., "Need this live in 30 days").
- **Weak Signals**:
  - Asking for generic demos.
  - Vague timing (e.g., "Maybe next year").
  - Technical-only feature questions.

## 2. Competitor Scoring
- **Favorable**: Competitor mentioned negatively or as an outdated system we are replacing.
- **Unfavorable**: Competitor mentioned as standard or preferred option by the prospect.

## 3. Recommended CRM Stages
- **Prospecting**: Initial contact, needs/pain point discovery.
- **Qualified**: Confirmed budget, timeline, and authority.
- **Proposal**: Custom presentation or quote delivered.
- **Negotiation**: Contract or price adjustments under discussion.
- **Closed Won**: Deal signed.
- **Closed Lost**: Competitor selected, project cancelled, or no decision.
